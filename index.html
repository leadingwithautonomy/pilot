About the Course

The course is based on exciting new research about how leaders can use autonomy to significantly increase employee commitment and generate successful results. The research uncovered significant disparities between what employees are looking for from their managers, and what the managers are providing. Leading with Autonomy translates this research into practical guidelines you can use on the job.

Learning will be experiential, with scenario-based activities focusing on the most frequent and realistic challenges you face as both managers and team leaders.

You will receive extensive resource materials, including expert tips and techniques for applying your learning from the workshop to your job.

Course Outline

The program consists of four sessions, each approximately two hours in length, that enable you to avoid micromanagement “triggers” and focus on the specific leadership skills that foster autonomy.

Session 1: Increasing Autonomy and Accountability
  • Why is creating autonomy so important?
  • Why does it seem so elusive?
  • How can you create the right level of autonomy for each of your employees?
Session 2: Overcoming Micromanagement
  • What makes even the best managers cross the line into micromanagement?
  • How can you identify what might cause you to micromanage?
  • What personal strategies can you use to avoid becoming an accidental micromanager?
Session 3: Setting Expectations to Create Autonomy and Accountability
  • How can expectation-setting, done right, set the stage for autonomy?
  • Why will ineffective expectation-setting virtually guarantee micromanagement?
  • How can you set expectations that reflect the needs of the different types and levels of your employees?
Session 4: Making Time for New Priorities
  • How can you use high-leverage management practices to balance your own work with what your boss and employees want and need from you?
  • How can you put together a personal implementation plan for leading with autonomy that is simple, effective, and measurable?
